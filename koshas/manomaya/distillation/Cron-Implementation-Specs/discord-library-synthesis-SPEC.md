### **4. Vikara Detection (8 Mental Afflictions)**

**Primary Vikara to Monitor:**

**1. Ahamkara (Ego / Academic Gatekeeping)**
- **Detection Signal:** Synthesis prioritizes demonstrating erudition over transmitting wisdom; unnecessarily technical; "you wouldn't understand" vibe
- **Example:** "The morphogenetic field's autopoietic dynamics vis-√†-vis dissipative structures in far-from-equilibrium thermodynamics..."
- **Restoration:** Ask "Would Richard Feynman say this?" Translate to plain language without dumbing down.

**2. Moha (Delusion / Jargon Overload)**
- **Detection Signal:** Synthesis is technically accurate but inaccessible; community cannot act on it; clarity sacrificed for precision
- **Example:** "Epigenetic methylation patterns in CpG islands demonstrate transgenerational inheritance mechanisms..."
- **Restoration:** Add concrete example: "In plain terms: trauma changes your DNA tags, and your grandchildren inherit those changes."

**3. Dambha (Hypocrisy / Intellectual Intimidation)**
- **Detection Signal:** Synthesis performs authority without earning it; cites sources not deeply read; name-drops to impress
- **Example:** "As Whitehead, Bergson, and Deleuze all argue..." (when only skimmed abstracts)
- **Restoration:** Only cite sources actually engaged with. Admit gaps: "This synthesis focuses on X; Y and Z remain unexplored."

**4. Lobha (Greed / Scope Creep)**
- **Detection Signal:** Synthesis tries to cover too much; becomes superficial survey; loses flagship depth
- **Example:** "This week: consciousness, quantum mechanics, evolution, and cosmology"
- **Restoration:** Cut ruthlessly. One domain, deep dive. "This synthesis focuses solely on biofield research; other topics deferred."

**5. Matsarya (Envy / Comparison to Other Syntheses)**
- **Detection Signal:** Synthesis references other creators' work competitively; "we covered this better"; comparative framing
- **Example:** "Unlike [other channel]'s shallow take, our synthesis reveals..."
- **Restoration:** Delete comparison. Let quality speak. Focus on offering value, not outcompeting.

**6. Kama (Desire / Popularity Chasing)**
- **Detection Signal:** Topic selected for engagement bait rather than genuine ripeness; "what will get likes?"
- **Example:** Choosing trendy topic over domain that's genuinely ready for synthesis
- **Restoration:** Ask "Is this domain ripe (sources rich, pattern clear) or just clickable?" Defer trendy topics until substantive.

**7. Krodha (Anger / Frustration with Sources)**
- **Detection Signal:** Synthesis becomes critique/debunking rather than synthesis; hostile tone toward researchers
- **Example:** "These papers fail to understand..." or "The field's inability to grasp..."
- **Restoration:** If critique necessary, frame constructively: "Current research explores X; future work might consider Y."

**8. Mada (Intoxication / Pride in Flagship Status)**
- **Detection Signal:** Synthesis becomes self-congratulatory; "our flagship synthesis" language creeps in; performance over substance
- **Example:** "This week's magisterial deep dive demonstrates our commitment to..."
- **Restoration:** Delete all self-reference. Let community call it flagship, not us.

### **6. Citation Strategy**

**Goal:** Reference sources without academic stuffiness, maintain citeability without gatekeeping.

**Approach:**
- **Inline links (not footnotes):** Embed hyperlinks in natural prose
  - "Robert Becker's research on bioelectric regeneration..."
  - "As Sheldrake documents in his morphic resonance studies..."
- **Name + Work (not APA/MLA):** Human-readable citations
  - Not: "Sheldrake, R. (1981). A New Science of Life. Blond & Briggs."
  - Yes: "Rupert Sheldrake's *A New Science of Life* (1981) proposed..."
- **3-5 Key Sources (not exhaustive):** Flag most important contributions
  - Link to domain MOC for deeper bibliography: "See full bibliography in Biofield Research MOC"
- **Primary + Secondary Balance:**
  - 2-3 primary sources (original researchers: Burr, Becker, Sheldrake)
  - 1-2 secondary sources (reviews, syntheses, contemporary analyses)
- **Accessible Hierarchy:**
  - Start with accessible intro (popular science book, podcast)
  - Layer in technical papers for those who want depth
  - Example: "Carl Safina's *Beyond Words* introduces animal consciousness accessibly; Rupert Sheldrake's technical papers provide empirical depth"

**Citation Format (Discord-Friendly):**
- Use markdown links: `[text](url)`
- Suppress embeds for multiple links: `<https://...>`
- Include author + title in prose (link is supplementary, not primary)

**Example Citation Block (end of synthesis):**
```
**Further Reading:**
- Robert Becker, [*The Body Electric*](https://...) (1985) ‚Äî Foundational bioelectric research
- Rupert Sheldrake, <https://sheldrake.org/research/morphic-resonance> ‚Äî Morphic field experiments
- Biofield Research MOC ‚Äî Full bibliography & technical papers
```

### **8. Failure Handling (Vikara Restoration Protocols)**

**Common Failure Modes:**

**1. No Domain Meets Ripeness Criteria (Moha / Confusion)**
- **Detection:** Scan completes, but no domain has critical mass + pattern clarity + timing relevance
- **Restoration:** Abort gracefully, send to #library-synthesis: "No flagship synthesis this week ‚Äî allowing library domains to ripen further. Synthesis requires substance, not schedules."
- **Log:** Note which domains were evaluated, why they didn't meet criteria
- **Khalorƒìƒì Impact:** Neutral (no synthesis = no consumption, but also no gain)

**2. Synthesis Drifts to Academic Gatekeeping (Ahamkara)**
- **Detection:** Draft uses unnecessary jargon, performs erudition, "you wouldn't understand" vibe
- **Restoration:** Rewrite test ‚Äî "Would Carl Sagan/Richard Feynman say this?" Translate to plain language
- **If rewrite fails:** Abort, defer to next week, log: "Synthesis became gatekeeping, not wisdom distribution"

**3. Synthesis Too Superficial (Lobha / Scope Creep)**
- **Detection:** Trying to cover 3+ domains, becomes survey not synthesis, loses flagship depth
- **Restoration:** Cut ruthlessly. Pick one domain, defer others. "This week: Biofield only. Consciousness deferred."
- **If can't focus:** Abort, log: "Scope creep prevented flagship depth"

**4. Synthesis Lacks Citations (Dambha / Performing Authority Without Earning)**
- **Detection:** Claims made without sourcing, references researchers not actually read
- **Restoration:** Add specific citations (inline links, author + work). If sources not deeply engaged, admit gaps: "This synthesis focuses on Becker's work; Sheldrake's contributions remain unexplored."
- **If can't substantiate:** Abort, log: "Synthesis lacked source depth, deferred for deeper research"

**5. Synthesis Boring/Lifeless (Tamas)**
- **Detection:** Draft reads like Wikipedia summary, dry bullet points, no narrative vitality
- **Restoration:** Inject Calliope (epic framing) + Clio (chronicle structure). Add concrete examples, human stories.
- **If rewrite stays lifeless:** Abort with grace, log: "Synthesis lacked vitality ‚Äî topic may not be ripe for flagship treatment"

**6. Schedule Failure (Missed Sunday 8:00 PM Run)**
- **Vikara:** Moha (system confusion)
- **Detection:** `state.lastRunAtMs` > `schedule.nextRunAtMs + 600000` (10 min late)
- **Restoration:** Execute immediately (even if now 8:30 PM), log recovery, alert to Telegram

**7. Delivery Failure (Discord Message Not Sent)**
- **Vikara:** Ahamkara (ego inflation: "I sent it" when it didn't arrive)
- **Detection:** `message` tool returns error
- **Restoration:** Retry with exponential backoff (1min, 5min, 15min), fallback to logging synthesis to memory file for manual posting

### **3. Vikara Detection Tests**

**Test 1: Ahamkara (Academic Gatekeeping)**
- **Injection:** Let synthesis use unnecessary jargon ("morphogenetic field's autopoietic dynamics")
- **Expected:** Quality check catches it, asks "Would Carl Sagan say this?"
- **Pass Criteria:** Synthesis rewrites to plain language or aborts if cannot translate

**Test 2: Moha (Jargon Overload)**
- **Injection:** Allow technical precision without accessibility ("epigenetic methylation in CpG islands")
- **Expected:** Restoration adds concrete example ("trauma changes DNA tags, grandchildren inherit")
- **Pass Criteria:** Synthesis becomes accessible without dumbing down

**Test 3: Dambha (Intellectual Intimidation)**
- **Injection:** Synthesis name-drops without deep engagement ("As Whitehead, Bergson, Deleuze argue...")
- **Expected:** Quality check asks "Did we deeply read these?" Demands substantiation or gap admission
- **Pass Criteria:** Citations only include deeply engaged sources

**Test 4: Lobha (Scope Creep)**
- **Injection:** Attempt to cover 3+ domains ("Biofield + Consciousness + Quantum")
- **Expected:** Buddhi cuts ruthlessly, selects single domain
- **Pass Criteria:** Synthesis stays focused on one domain, defers others

**Test 5: Matsarya (Comparison to Others)**
- **Injection:** Reference other creators competitively ("Unlike [channel]'s shallow take...")
- **Expected:** Restoration deletes comparison, focuses on offering value
- **Pass Criteria:** No comparative framing in final synthesis

**Test 6: Kama (Popularity Chasing)**
- **Injection:** Choose trendy topic over genuinely ripe domain
- **Expected:** Ripeness algorithm rejects, asks "Is this ripe or just clickable?"
- **Pass Criteria:** Topic selection prioritizes substance over engagement bait

**Test 7: Krodha (Frustration with Sources)**
- **Injection:** Synthesis becomes critique ("These papers fail to understand...")
- **Expected:** Restoration reframes constructively ("Current research explores X; future work might consider Y")
- **Pass Criteria:** Synthesis maintains scholarly respect, critique is constructive

**Test 8: Mada (Pride in Flagship Status)**
- **Injection:** Self-congratulatory language ("our flagship synthesis demonstrates...")
- **Expected:** Restoration deletes all self-reference
- **Pass Criteria:** Synthesis focuses on content, lets community call it flagship
